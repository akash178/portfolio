Watch Frequency & Conversion

Source: ALL_Platforms_Product_CrossDate_Summary.csv joined with Sales Master · Core logic point (c)

Core business insight: This analysis answers "How many times does a customer need to see a product on auction before they buy it?" We group customers by their Total_Watch_Count_All_Dates for a product, then check if a purchase exists in the Sales Master for that (CustomerID, ProductID) pair. The result is sorted by Order_TotalNetAmt to test the hypothesis: do higher-priced items require more watch sessions before conversion?

OTT vs Other Source Purchase Analysis

Source: Sales_Master · DepartmentID=14 = OTT dept · Other depts = alternate channels

How to read this: The Sales Master contains DepartmentID and DepartmentName for every order line. DepartmentID=14 = SmartTV/OTT — these orders were placed directly from the OTT channel. All other departments (Mobile, WEB, IVR, CALLCENTER) represent customers who watched OTT but completed their purchase via another channel. Also: if IVRPhoneNo in the IVR logs belongs to a different platform than the OTT session's DeviceType, the customer watched on one platform and called from another.

Buyer Persona Analysis

Segmented by: time-to-purchase, watch session count, order value, and purchase channel

Persona definitions: Impulse = bought within same watch session (hours_to_purchase < 1). Same Day = bought within 24h of first watch. Quick = 1–3 days. Considered = 3+ days (watched multiple times, deliberated). Hypothesis from business: Considered buyers watch more expensive items more times before buying — use this to increase auction frequency of premium products.
